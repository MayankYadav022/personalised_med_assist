Neurological Symptoms: Recognizing Serious Signs

Neurological symptoms can indicate conditions ranging from tension headaches to life-threatening strokes. Recognizing warning signs is crucial for timely intervention.

Stroke Warning Signs - Remember FAST:
- Face: Ask the person to smile. Does one side of the face droop?
- Arms: Ask the person to raise both arms. Does one arm drift downward?
- Speech: Ask the person to repeat a phrase. Is their speech slurred or strange?
- Time: If you observe any of these signs, call emergency services immediately.

Additional stroke symptoms include sudden severe headache, sudden vision changes, sudden trouble walking, dizziness, and loss of balance.

Seizures:
Seizures are sudden, uncontrolled electrical disturbances in the brain. Symptoms vary from staring spells to convulsions with loss of consciousness. If someone has a seizure:
- Clear the area of dangerous objects
- Do not restrain the person
- Do not put anything in their mouth
- Time the seizure
- Call emergency services if it lasts more than 5 minutes

Headaches:
Most headaches are tension headaches or migraines. However, seek emergency care for:
- Sudden, severe "thunderclap" headache
- Headache with fever, stiff neck, and rash
- Headache after head injury
- Headache with confusion, weakness, or vision changes
- "Worst headache of your life"

Migraines are severe headaches often accompanied by nausea, vomiting, and sensitivity to light and sound. They may be preceded by visual disturbances called aura.

Other Neurological Conditions:
Meningitis is inflammation of the membranes surrounding the brain and spinal cord. Symptoms include severe headache, fever, stiff neck, and sensitivity to light. Bacterial meningitis is a medical emergency.

Multiple sclerosis is a chronic condition affecting the central nervous system. Symptoms include numbness, weakness, vision problems, and coordination difficulties.

When to Seek Medical Care:
Any sudden neurological symptom, severe headache, or changes in consciousness require immediate medical evaluation.
