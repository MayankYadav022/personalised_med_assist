Pediatric Symptoms: When to Seek Care for Children

Children often experience symptoms differently than adults. Knowing when to seek medical care for your child is important for their health and safety.

Fever in Children:
Fever is a common symptom in children and often indicates infection. For infants under 3 months, any fever (100.4°F or 38°C or higher) requires immediate medical attention. For older children, seek care if:
- Fever lasts more than 3 days
- Fever is very high (above 104°F or 40°C)
- Child appears very ill, lethargic, or irritable
- Fever is accompanied by rash, stiff neck, or difficulty breathing

Respiratory Symptoms in Children:
Children commonly experience colds and respiratory infections. Watch for:
- Rapid breathing or difficulty breathing
- Bluish color around lips or face
- Wheezing or grunting sounds
- Persistent cough
- Refusal to eat or drink

Croup causes a distinctive barking cough and hoarse voice. Severe croup may require emergency care if the child has difficulty breathing.

Gastrointestinal Symptoms:
Vomiting and diarrhea are common in children. Seek medical care if:
- Signs of dehydration (dry mouth, no tears, decreased urination)
- Vomiting persists for more than 24 hours
- Blood in vomit or stool
- Severe abdominal pain
- Child refuses fluids

Rashes in Children:
Many childhood illnesses cause rashes. Chickenpox causes itchy blisters. Measles causes red-brown spots. Hand, foot, and mouth disease causes sores in the mouth and rash on hands and feet.

Seek emergency care for rash with fever, especially if the child appears very ill.

Common Childhood Illnesses:
Ear infections cause ear pain, fever, and irritability. Strep throat causes severe sore throat, fever, and swollen glands. Urinary tract infections may cause fever, pain with urination, and abdominal pain.

When to Seek Emergency Care:
- Difficulty breathing
- Severe injury
- Seizure
- Loss of consciousness
- Severe allergic reaction (swelling, difficulty breathing, hives)
- Dehydration
- High fever in infants

Trust your instincts. If you are concerned about your child's symptoms, contact your healthcare provider.
