Cardiac Symptoms and When to Seek Emergency Care

Chest pain is one of the most common symptoms that brings people to the emergency room. While not all chest pain is related to the heart, it's important to recognize warning signs of a potential heart attack.

Heart Attack Warning Signs:
- Chest pain or pressure that lasts more than a few minutes or comes and goes
- Pain or discomfort in one or both arms, back, neck, jaw, or stomach
- Shortness of breath with or without chest discomfort
- Cold sweats, nausea, or lightheadedness
- Sudden weakness or fatigue

If you experience these symptoms, call emergency services immediately. Do not drive yourself to the hospital.

Angina is chest pain caused by reduced blood flow to the heart. It may feel like pressure or squeezing in your chest. Stable angina occurs during physical activity or stress and goes away with rest. Unstable angina is more dangerous and can occur even at rest.

Other Cardiac Conditions:
Arrhythmias are irregular heartbeats. Symptoms include palpitations, fluttering in the chest, dizziness, and fainting. Some arrhythmias are harmless, while others can be life-threatening.

Heart failure occurs when the heart cannot pump enough blood to meet the body's needs. Symptoms include shortness of breath, fatigue, swollen legs, and rapid heartbeat.

Risk Factors for Heart Disease:
- High blood pressure
- High cholesterol
- Smoking
- Diabetes
- Obesity
- Family history of heart disease
- Age (men over 45, women over 55)
- Sedentary lifestyle

Prevention includes regular exercise, healthy diet, maintaining healthy weight, not smoking, and managing stress.
