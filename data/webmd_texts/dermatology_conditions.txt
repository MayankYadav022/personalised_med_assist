Dermatology Conditions: Skin Problems and When to See a Specialist

Skin conditions range from minor irritations to serious diseases requiring specialized care. Understanding common skin problems helps determine when to seek treatment.

Common Skin Conditions:
Acne is a skin condition causing pimples, blackheads, and whiteheads. It occurs when hair follicles become clogged with oil and dead skin cells. Treatment includes topical medications, oral antibiotics, and lifestyle changes.

Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis) causes dry, itchy, inflamed skin. It's common in children but can occur at any age. Triggers include irritants, allergens, stress, and temperature changes. Treatment involves moisturizers and anti-inflammatory creams.

Psoriasis is an autoimmune condition causing rapid skin cell growth, resulting in thick, scaly patches. It commonly affects the elbows, knees, scalp, and lower back. Treatment includes topical treatments, light therapy, and systemic medications.

Rashes:
Rashes can be caused by allergies, infections, medications, or autoimmune conditions. Most rashes are harmless and resolve on their own. However, seek medical care for:
- Rash covering large areas of the body
- Rash with fever
- Painful rash
- Blistering rash
- Rash that doesn't improve within a week

Skin Infections:
Bacterial infections like cellulitis cause red, swollen, painful skin and require antibiotics. Fungal infections cause itching, redness, and scaling. Viral infections like shingles cause painful blisters along nerve pathways.

Serious Skin Warning Signs:
Changes in moles or new growths may indicate skin cancer. Remember ABCDE:
- Asymmetry: One half doesn't match the other
- Border: Irregular, scalloped, or poorly defined edges
- Color: Varied colors including brown, black, red, white, or blue
- Diameter: Larger than 6mm (pencil eraser)
- Evolving: Changes in size, shape, or color

When to See a Dermatologist:
- Persistent skin problems not responding to over-the-counter treatments
- Suspicious moles or growths
- Severe acne
- Chronic skin conditions like eczema or psoriasis
- Hair loss
- Nail problems
- Skin cancer screening

Prevention:
Protect skin from sun exposure with sunscreen, protective clothing, and shade. Maintain good hygiene, moisturize regularly, and avoid known irritants.
