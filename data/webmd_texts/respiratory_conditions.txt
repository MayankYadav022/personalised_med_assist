Respiratory Conditions: From Common Cold to Serious Infections

The respiratory system can be affected by various conditions ranging from mild to life-threatening. Understanding symptoms helps determine when to seek medical care.

Common Cold:
Symptoms include runny nose, sneezing, sore throat, mild cough, and mild fatigue. Colds typically last 7-10 days and resolve on their own with rest and fluids.

Influenza (Flu):
Flu symptoms are more severe than cold symptoms and include high fever (often above 100.4°F or 38°C), body aches, severe fatigue, headache, and dry cough. Flu can lead to complications like pneumonia, especially in elderly, young children, and those with weakened immune systems.

Pneumonia:
This lung infection causes inflammation in the air sacs. Symptoms include high fever, chills, cough with phlegm, chest pain when breathing, and shortness of breath. Pneumonia requires medical treatment with antibiotics (for bacterial pneumonia) or antivirals.

Asthma:
Asthma is a chronic condition causing airway inflammation. Symptoms include wheezing, shortness of breath, chest tightness, and coughing. Asthma attacks can be triggered by allergens, exercise, cold air, or respiratory infections.

Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD):
COPD includes emphysema and chronic bronchitis. Symptoms include persistent cough, increased mucus production, shortness of breath, and wheezing. COPD is primarily caused by smoking.

When to Seek Emergency Care:
- Severe difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
- Bluish lips or face
- Chest pain
- Confusion or altered mental state
- High fever with severe symptoms
- Coughing up blood

For mild respiratory symptoms, rest, hydration, and over-the-counter medications may help. However, persistent symptoms lasting more than a week or worsening symptoms require medical evaluation.
