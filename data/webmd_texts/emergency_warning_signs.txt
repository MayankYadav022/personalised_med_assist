Emergency Warning Signs: When to Call for Immediate Help

Certain symptoms require immediate medical attention. Knowing these warning signs can save lives.

Call Emergency Services (911) for:

Heart Attack:
- Chest pain or pressure lasting more than a few minutes
- Pain spreading to arms, back, neck, or jaw
- Shortness of breath
- Cold sweats, nausea, or lightheadedness

Stroke:
- Face drooping on one side
- Arm weakness or numbness
- Speech difficulty
- Sudden severe headache
- Sudden vision changes
- Sudden trouble walking

Severe Allergic Reaction (Anaphylaxis):
- Difficulty breathing or wheezing
- Swelling of face, lips, or throat
- Rapid heartbeat
- Dizziness or fainting
- Hives or widespread rash

Severe Bleeding:
- Bleeding that won't stop with pressure
- Blood spurting from wound
- Signs of shock (pale, clammy skin, rapid heartbeat)

Seizures:
- Seizure lasting more than 5 minutes
- Repeated seizures without recovery
- First-time seizure
- Seizure in someone who is pregnant or diabetic

Severe Burns:
- Burns covering large areas
- Burns on face, hands, feet, or genitals
- Chemical or electrical burns

Poisoning:
- Suspected ingestion of toxic substance
- Difficulty breathing
- Drowsiness or confusion
- Chemical burns around mouth

Heat Stroke:
- High body temperature (above 103°F or 39.4°C)
- Hot, red, dry skin
- Rapid pulse
- Confusion or unconsciousness

Hypothermia:
- Body temperature below 95°F (35°C)
- Shivering, confusion, drowsiness
- Slow breathing or heartbeat

Severe Injuries:
- Head injury with loss of consciousness
- Suspected broken bones
- Deep wounds
- Back or neck injury

For Infants and Children:
- Any fever in infants under 3 months
- Difficulty breathing
- Bluish skin color
- Severe lethargy or unresponsiveness
- Signs of dehydration

When in Doubt:
If you are unsure whether a situation is an emergency, it's better to seek medical evaluation. Trust your instincts. Emergency departments are equipped to assess and treat urgent medical conditions.

Before Help Arrives:
- Stay calm
- Keep the person comfortable
- Do not move injured persons unless necessary
- Apply first aid if trained
- Gather information about what happened
