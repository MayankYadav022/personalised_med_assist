Gastrointestinal Issues: When to Worry About Stomach Problems

Gastrointestinal (GI) symptoms are common and usually resolve on their own. However, certain symptoms require medical attention.

Common GI Symptoms:
Abdominal pain can have many causes including indigestion, gas, constipation, or more serious conditions. The location and nature of pain help determine the cause.

Nausea and vomiting often accompany infections, food poisoning, motion sickness, or medication side effects. Persistent vomiting can lead to dehydration.

Diarrhea is characterized by loose, watery stools. Acute diarrhea usually resolves within a few days. Chronic diarrhea lasting more than four weeks requires medical evaluation.

Constipation involves infrequent bowel movements or difficulty passing stools. It's often caused by low fiber intake, dehydration, or lack of physical activity.

Serious GI Symptoms Requiring Medical Care:
- Severe abdominal pain that doesn't improve
- Blood in stool or black, tarry stools
- Persistent vomiting or vomiting blood
- Unexplained weight loss
- Difficulty swallowing
- Persistent heartburn
- Yellowing of skin or eyes (jaundice)

Gastroenteritis (Stomach Flu):
This infection of the stomach and intestines causes diarrhea, vomiting, stomach cramps, and sometimes fever. It's usually caused by viruses and resolves within a few days. Hydration is essential.

Peptic Ulcers:
Ulcers are sores in the stomach lining or small intestine. Symptoms include burning stomach pain, bloating, and nausea. Treatment involves antibiotics (for H. pylori infection) and acid-reducing medications.

Gallbladder Problems:
Gallstones can cause severe pain in the upper right abdomen, especially after fatty meals. Symptoms may include nausea, vomiting, and fever.

Appendicitis:
Appendicitis causes pain that starts near the belly button and moves to the lower right abdomen. Other symptoms include fever, nausea, and loss of appetite. This is a surgical emergency.

When to Seek Emergency Care:
- Severe abdominal pain with fever
- Rigid or swollen abdomen
- Inability to pass stool or gas
- Signs of dehydration from vomiting or diarrhea
- Blood in vomit or stool
